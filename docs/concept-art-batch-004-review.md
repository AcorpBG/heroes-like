## Preliminary Curation

Batch-level read:

- Accept as external concept evidence only. These images are useful for visual direction and later implementation-brief inputs, but none should be treated as final art, final UI, map data, faction logo, unit reference, or runtime/source asset.
- Batch 004 completes the main named second-pass faction/town/unit gaps that remained after batch 003: Mireclaw town/object, Veilmourn town/unit, and Sunvault seven-tier ladder.
- Implementation-brief prep should still wait for AcOrP curation and at least one broader overworld object/town study pass covering route-law objects, resource sites, faction landmarks, neutral encounter presentation, guarded sites, pickups, footprints, and approach sides.
- The strongest production value in this batch is functional infrastructure: Mireclaw chain settlements and drum platforms, Veilmourn bell harbor and mirror drydock language, and Sunvault logistics crews plus large relay/battery silhouettes.

Mireclaw town and object study:

- Works: the main settlement reads as low, wet, mobile, and marsh-controlled rather than as a walled town. Chain bridges, floating platforms, ferry booms, reed islands, drum towers, hidden slips, bone stakes, peat blocks, mudglass chunks, shrine posts, and den pens strongly support Drowned Marches sovereignty.
- Works: the object studies are unusually useful for overworld classification. Drum huts, ferry platforms, chain posts, reed/bone markers, peat cuts, carts, mudglass deposits, and beast pens can become compact site families with readable interaction or decoration roles.
- Risks: the large settlement is too detailed and horizontally sprawling for direct 3x2 overworld use. Future briefs must reduce it into a compact silhouette with a clear approach tile and avoid copying the exact dock layout.
- Risks: bone stakes, den beasts, red cloth, and murky palette can slide back toward swamp-horror or generic raider visuals if ferry law, shrine drums, peat economy, and wetland logistics are not kept explicit.
- Decision: keep as strong Mireclaw town/object readiness evidence after AcOrP review. Defer unit-ladder approval; this sheet helps object and town identity more than tactical tier silhouettes.

Veilmourn town and unit ladder study:

- Works: the town side strongly supports harbor-inside-fog identity. Bell towers, mirror-water piers, black lacquered boats, hanging lanterns, funeral cloth, mirror drydock geometry, fog gaps, mooring chains, and warm lamp points give Veilmourn a coherent town and overworld landmark language.
- Works: the unit/object side gives useful props for game-scale silhouettes: bells, lantern standards, chart boards, chain hooks, harpoons, mirror shields, maskglass gear, obituary robes, and a large fog-walker or vessel-like capstone.
- Risks: the architecture is elegant enough to drift toward gothic church or fantasy harbor if obituary, salvage law, charts, bell buoys, fog lanes, and black-sail logistics are not kept primary.
- Risks: several humanoid silhouettes still share similar coat, hat, and lantern profiles. The ladder needs stronger tier spacing before combat sprite or animation briefs, especially between scout, lantern bearer, corsair, scribe, and reaver roles.
- Decision: keep as Veilmourn town/object and first unit-ladder evidence. Defer final unit approval until AcOrP decides whether the silhouette spread is distinct enough or needs a stricter black-shape tier pass.

Sunvault seven-tier silhouette study:

- Works: this is the most production-useful Sunvault unit pass so far. The sheet foregrounds survey tools, calibration posts, crystal carts, lens tripods, prism adepts, chorister support groups, crane rigs, mirror panels, array carts, aurora batteries, and large relay machinery instead of relying only on armored champions.
- Works: the scale progression is clear. The early tiers read as surveyor/warden crews, middle tiers as support and crystal logistics, and upper tiers as protected batteries and large relay constructs. The black silhouette strips are useful for checking battlefield readability and animation workload.
- Risks: some shield and spear variants still retain soldier-order pressure, and the pale stone/gold/blue-violet palette can become holy if future briefs remove the engineering and rigging props.
- Risks: several rows show equipment crews rather than single combat units. Future implementation briefs must decide whether Sunvault high tiers are multi-figure crews, construct wagons, or single large battlefield pieces before JSON/combat sprite planning.
- Decision: keep as useful Sunvault seven-tier direction and likely sufficient to move out of the anti-paladin recalibration loop after AcOrP review. Do not treat the icon shields, palette swatches, or exact machinery silhouettes as final production designs.
