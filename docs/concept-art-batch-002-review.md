## Preliminary Curation

Batch-level read:

- Accept as external concept evidence only. These images are useful for direction, but none should be treated as final art, final UI, map data, faction logo, or unit reference.
- Batch 002 improves the specific problems it targeted: the world study is more route-readable, the Veilmourn revision removes embedded text, and Sunvault gains more calibration tools and infrastructure props.
- It is still not enough evidence to begin broad implementation briefs. Thornwake, Brasshollow, Embercourt town/object, Mireclaw town/object, Veilmourn town/unit, and Sunvault tier-ladder follow-ups remain needed before the art direction is balanced across all six factions.

World route readability:

- Works: strong central hub, clear branching roads, repeated bridges, readable river crossings, separate crystal/furnace/root/fog zones, visible rails, and map-object clusters make this much more useful for adventure-map route thinking than the first world mood image.
- Risks: the image is still a finished panorama with very high object density. Several visitable-looking sites, relays, crystals, and landmarks would collapse at live strategy scale unless reduced into a strict object taxonomy and density sheet.
- Decision: keep as route-readability evidence and as a useful Ninefold Confluence mood direction. Do not use as direct map art. Next world pass should split this into smaller region-density studies with explicit object footprints, approach sides, and negative space.

Veilmourn no-text revision:

- Works: the no-text constraint is satisfied. Lantern buoys, hanging bells, black-lacquered boats, pale fog cloth, cracked mirror charts, salvage tools, salt-stained stone, warm lamps, and harbor/vault structures align well with the Veilmourn brief.
- Risks: some figures can still drift toward generic rugged coastal adventurers or dark rogues if the bell, chart, obituary, salvage-law, and memory-salt cues are removed. The boats could become pirate-adjacent if black sail and weaponry dominate later prompts.
- Decision: keep as the strongest Veilmourn direction so far for materials, props, boats, and coast objects. Request a follow-up Veilmourn town exterior plus a silhouette-first unit ladder that makes salvage-house law, bells, memory charts, and fog-route control visible at game scale.

Sunvault silhouette study:

- Works: lens tripods, calibration rigs, crystal carts, relay towers, prism roads, measuring staffs, shield lenses, and support crews support the Sunvault as engineers of light, sightlines, and crystal infrastructure rather than a pure temple faction.
- Risks: the humanoid armor, tall staffs, shields, capes, and pale-gold palette still carry holy knight or paladin pressure in several variants. The strongest production value is in the tools and infrastructure, not the armored champion silhouettes.
- Decision: keep for Sunvault object, building, and support-role language. Defer unit approval. The next Sunvault pass should be a true seven-tier silhouette ladder with stronger worker, operator, choir-math, relay-maintenance, and crystal-engineer identity, and with fewer knightly helmets, capes, and heraldic shield reads.
