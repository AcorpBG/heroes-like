## Preliminary Curation

Batch-level read:

- Accept as external concept evidence only. These images are useful for visual direction and implementation-brief inputs later, but none should be treated as final art, final UI, map data, faction logo, unit reference, or runtime/source asset.
- Batch 003 materially improves second-pass coverage for Thornwake, Brasshollow, and Embercourt. It still does not complete the full second-pass expansion because Mireclaw town/object, Veilmourn town/unit, Sunvault seven-tier ladder, and broader object/town studies remain missing.
- The strongest production value is in object and town infrastructure vocabulary: root gates, graft racks, bramble rings, rail beds, quarry gantries, furnace doors, lock gates, beacon towers, cranes, mills, and water-control structures.
- Any implementation brief derived from this batch must translate the useful motifs into original game-scale silhouettes, explicit footprints, approach sides, animation hooks, and rejection constraints rather than copying the rendered sheets.

Thornwake silhouette, town, and object study:

- Works: the sheet makes Thornwake read more as root law and living infrastructure than as a simple elf/druid faction. Thorn toll keepers, graft tenders, living-road surveyors, root-law wardens, amber lanterns, root gates, nursery racks, bramble enclosures, and living-road town diagrams all support the Concord brief.
- Works: the town/object direction is stronger than the humanoid costume direction. The best pieces are the large root gate, nursery rack, amber root lanterns, thorn toll arch, bramble ring, and bottom-row town-route studies that show roads being redirected by living roots.
- Risks: the sheet includes embedded labels and banner-like marks. Treat those only as temporary callouts; do not carry text, symbols, or pseudo-heraldry into implementation.
- Risks: some robed masked figures, pale branch shields, and ceremonial black/white treatment can drift toward funerary cult, generic dark order, or forest-guardian reads if root-law, toll, graft, and renewal-debt props are removed.
- Decision: keep as useful Thornwake town/object and support-silhouette evidence. Defer unit-ladder approval. A later Thornwake unit ladder still needs tier-to-tier silhouette progression from seedcutters through Worldroot Bastion, with less reliance on robed human variants.

Brasshollow silhouette, town, and object study:

- Works: the object vocabulary is strong for production planning. Furnace doors, pressure rails, quarry cutaways, gantries, drill platforms, pipe columns, gauges, heat vents, slag/cracked tiles, banner standards, and small industrial markers give Brasshollow a clear map-object and building kit.
- Works: the silhouettes are readable at a broad shape level: armored labor, pressure tool carrier, shield/pavis body, gauge staff, hammer workbench, and heavy marshal. The black silhouette row is useful for checking mass, posture, and prop profiles.
- Risks: the humanoid figures still lean toward generic armored industrial soldiers. The dwarf risk is reduced by human proportions, but decorative steampunk pressure remains high because glowing goggles, brass trim, backpacks, hoses, and heroic armor dominate.
- Risks: the image does not yet visibly prove Brasshollow's contract-law, debt, repair-window, worker-court, or audited-furnace identity. Without clause tablets, stamped contract metal, debt seals, service tags, or repair constraints, the faction could become "brass machine army."
- Decision: keep for Brasshollow object, building, route, and mine-site briefs. Defer unit approval. The next Brasshollow pass should be stricter about labor-contract systems, machine maintenance, debt seals, and capital-project silhouettes, with less heroic soldier framing.

Embercourt town and object study:

- Works: this is the strongest Embercourt town/object evidence so far. The large river-lock complex clearly communicates civic water control, guarded crossings, bridge spans, lock gates, weirs, beacon towers, cranes, docks, mills, barges, granary structures, and red signal accents.
- Works: the sheet supports both overworld-town and town-screen planning. The central town can become a large world landmark, while the surrounding object studies can inform beacon towers, lock mechanisms, barge cranes, mill islands, toll platforms, and granary/warehouse structures.
- Risks: the central composition is too large and polished to use directly. A game implementation needs a simplified 3x2 overworld town silhouette, a clear bottom/side approach tile, compact object footprints, and negative space around roads and water lanes.
- Risks: some roof shapes and ornament could drift toward generic river citadel or non-specific East/West civic fantasy if ash writs, public toll law, court bells, ration/granary logic, and beacon infrastructure are not kept explicit.
- Decision: keep as strong Embercourt town/object readiness evidence. Embercourt can likely move toward implementation-brief prep for town exterior and object-family language after AcOrP curation, while unit and hero art still need their own silhouette passes.
